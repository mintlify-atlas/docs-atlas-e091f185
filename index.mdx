KMP-NativeCoroutines bridges the gap between Kotlin Coroutines and Swift by generating native wrappers for your suspend functions and Flows. It adds what Kotlin's built-in ObjC interop lacks: proper cancellation and generic Flow types that work seamlessly across Swift Concurrency, Combine, and RxSwift.

## Why KMP-NativeCoroutines?

Kotlin Coroutines expose suspend functions to Swift as completion-handler callbacks — which don't support cancellation. Flows lose their generic type entirely in ObjC. KMP-NativeCoroutines solves both problems with a lightweight code generation approach.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the Gradle plugin">
    Apply `com.rickclephas.kmp.nativecoroutines` to your KMP module and KSP generates native wrapper code automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Annotate your Kotlin declarations">
    Mark suspend functions and Flow properties with `@NativeCoroutines` or `@NativeCoroutinesState`. The plugin handles the rest.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Call from Swift">
    Use `asyncFunction(for:)`, `createPublisher(for:)`, or `createObservable(for:)` to wrap the generated natives into idiomatic Swift types.
  </Step>
</Steps>
